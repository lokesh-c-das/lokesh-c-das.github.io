Welcome!

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science at the University of Memphis. My research focuses on developing novel algorithms for the next-generation traffic control system for autonomous vehicles (AVs) based on reinforcement learning. The primary objective of my research is to alleviate traffic congestion in critical congested areas, with the aim of optimizing traffic flow, road safety, and driving comfort simultaneously. I develop algorithms for self-driving cars to operate safely in various safety-critical highway scenarios, including highways with ramps and roundabouts.

I am passionate about researching and developing new algorithms based on AI and ML for intelligent autonomous cars (or autonomous robots) that can efficiently interact with the real-world environment without human intervention. I have extensive experience designing and developing ML algorithms, especially reinforcement learning algorithms. In my research, I design and formulate autonomous vehicle decision-making tasks as Markov decision processes (e.g., MDP, POMDP) and solve the problems using deep Q-Network (DQN), deep deterministic policy gradient (DDPG), proximal policy optimization (PPO), and actor/critic methods.
